The Thermal Pools of Iceland

Using data from an earlier map from the Icelandic Institute of Natural History, alongside information from the books Thermal Pools in Iceland by Jón G. Snæland and Þóra Sigurbjörnsdóttir and Geysers and Hot Springs in Iceland by Björn Hróarsson and Sigurður Sveinn Jónsson, I have compiled a map of the hot springs most relevant to travelers in Iceland. The map includes important information concerning access and bathing, so that users can make informed plans about which pools to visit. The hot springs are classified by their Icelandic names: hver, referring to pools that are too hot for a swim, laug, those best suited for bathing, and volgra, those which might be a bit too chilly for a dip.

The map, displayed below in an ArcGIS Story Map, is at this point only suited for viewing digitally, but future augmentations could see it developed into a print suited for use on the road and in the field.
